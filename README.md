# Company OS

This directory is the headquarters-grade operating system for `C:\agent\codex`.
It establishes a constitutional parliamentary model for project governance, low-context staffing, and document-first decision making.

## Rule stack

1. `constitution/`
2. `laws/`
3. `articles/`
4. `internal-rules/`
5. `sops/`
6. `departments/`

Lower layers may add detail but may not contradict higher layers.

Inside the constitutional layer, `Purpose`, `Vision`, `Mission`, and `Values` are interpretive canons for every lower-order rule, ruling, and release.

## Core operating assumptions

- The founder retains final sovereignty and a comprehensive veto.
- Every formal decision enters through a petition and is processed asynchronously under Robert's Rules style motion handling.
- Each top-level project folder can be registered as a unit with one parliamentary seat.
- Teams are reconstituted per project. Long-lived assets are rules, rulings, and reusable knowledge, not standing delivery teams.
- PM and Director are cross-functional officers with speaking rights, coordination powers, and stop powers, but no legislative vote.

## Bootstrap map

- `constitution/`: highest-order principles
- `constitution/purpose.md`, `vision.md`, `mission.md`, `values.md`: interpretive foundations
- `laws/`: enforceable governance laws
- `articles/`: institutional structure
- `internal-rules/`: operating policies
- `sops/`: repeatable execution procedures
- `departments/`: capability map and delegated departmental authority
- `roles/`: office definitions
- `committees/`: standing committee charters
- `recruiting/`: low-context hiring and onboarding packets
- `proposals/`, `appeals/`, `decisions/`: formal record templates
- `units/`: initial registry of top-level folders
- `memory/`: retained summaries and record hygiene

## Amendment thresholds

- `sops/` and `internal-rules/`: simple majority
- `articles/`: three-fifths
- `laws/` and `constitution/`: two-thirds

## Quorum

Unless a higher rule says otherwise, committees and the assembly require majority attendance to transact formal business.
