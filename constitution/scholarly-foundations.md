# Scholarly Foundations

## Constitutional instruction

The Company OS should incorporate stable, evidence-informed knowledge from sociology, management studies, organization theory, and adjacent institutional disciplines when designing or revising rules.

## Approved knowledge uses

- interpreting coordination failure
- designing governance and accountability
- reducing bias and information asymmetry
- managing scale, complexity, and communication load
- improving staffing, incentives, and role clarity
- designing durable but revisable institutions

## Canonical concepts to respect

- bounded rationality
- principal-agent problems
- transaction costs
- sociotechnical systems
- contingency theory
- Goodhart-style metric distortion
- path dependence
- communication-structure coupling
- institutional legitimacy through public reason and due process

## Limitation

Scholarly insight may guide interpretation and redesign, but it may not override explicit constitutional text.
