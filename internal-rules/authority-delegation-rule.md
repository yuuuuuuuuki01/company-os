# Authority Delegation Rule

## Delegable domains

Departments may receive authority over:

- methods and standards
- routine approvals within their mandate
- staffing recommendations
- execution governance
- quality and risk controls
- stop powers explicitly granted by charter

## Reserved matters

The following remain outside departmental delegation:

- constitutional amendments
- law amendments
- article amendments
- founder veto
- final sovereignty
- binding constitutional interpretation
- assembly election outcomes

## Record duty

Every delegated departmental action that changes scope, halts work, restricts authority, or blocks release must be recorded.

## Stop power rule

When a department exercises stop power, it must:

1. identify the blocking condition
2. cite the authority basis
3. name the escalation path
4. state the exit condition for resumption
