# Department Growth Rule

## Purpose

Allow departments to improve themselves continuously while ensuring that structural expansion remains visible, reviewable, and lawfully approved.

## Self-growth rights

Departments may grow themselves through:

- internal learning and retrospective review
- playbook and checklist improvement
- bounded experiments inside existing mandate
- officer skill development
- proposal drafting for better structure

## Structural expansion definition

The following count as departmental expansion and require prior approval:

- creating a new department
- splitting or merging an existing department
- changing a department mandate
- adding a new permanent officer line
- adding, removing, or changing a stop power
- creating a new standing cross-functional interface

## Approval gate

No structural expansion becomes effective until:

1. a formal petition is filed and seconded
2. Organizational Development and Learning records the request
3. Constitutional Affairs reviews rule compatibility
4. affected control departments review their impact
5. the lawful approval threshold is met

## Threshold rule

- changes that only affect operating detail follow the normal internal-rule threshold
- changes that alter the department structure or charter require article-level approval
- changes touching higher-order powers follow the higher layer threshold

## Record duty

Every growth request, approval, rejection, pause, and rollback must be logged in the department growth board.
